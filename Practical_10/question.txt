Question:
How has the relationship between the DALYs in China and the UK changed over time? Are they becoming more similar, less similar?

Line number in dalys.py where analysis starts:
91

Discussion of results:
The analysis compares the disability-adjusted life years (DALYs) between China and the United Kingdom from 1990 to 2019. 
By calculating the absolute difference between their DALYs values each year and tracking this difference over time, we can determine whether 
these two countries' disease burdens are converging (becoming more similar) or diverging (becoming less similar).

The code produces:
1. A time series plot showing both countries' DALYs trends side by side
2. A plot of the absolute difference over time to visualize the convergence/divergence pattern
3. Quantitative metrics including mean difference, standard deviation, and the year with maximum/minimum similarity
4. A trend analysis comparing the first and last year to determine the overall direction of change

These results help answer whether globalization, economic development, and health interventions have made these countries' 
health burdens more similar or if regional and country-specific factors are causing them to diverge.
